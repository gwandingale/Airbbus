Our History

1960s | Laying the Foundations

The 1960s marked the beginning of a new era in European aerospace with the collaboration of several nations to develop a competitive alternative to American manufacturers. This decade laid the groundwork for what would eventually become Airbus, with key partnerships forming and initial designs being conceptualized.

1969

Concorde takes off on its 1st flight in Toulouse

The Concorde prototype, a symbol of European engineering prowess.

1969

the first A300B built

Early design schematics for Airbus’s first commercial aircraft.

1969

The collaboration

Collaborations between France and Germany, pivotal in Airbus’s formation.

1965

The groundwork for Airbus began with discussions between France and Germany, setting the stage for a joint aerospace venture.

1969

The A300, Airbus’s first aircraft model, was officially launched, signaling the start of a new competitor in the global aerospace market.

1970s | European Collaboration

The 1970s were a decade of significant growth and expansion for Airbus. The official formation of Airbus Industrie marked the consolidation of European aerospace efforts, and the first flights of the A300 set new standards in aviation for efficiency and capacity.

1972

The world's first twin engine wide-body aircraft

The first A300B took to the skies, setting the stage for future innovations.

1974

A300B2 enters regular service for Air France

Initial commercial flights for Air France were from Paris to London.

1978

Eastern Airlines, Airbus' first US customer, orders 23 A300B4 aircraft

After the breakthrough agreement with Eastern Airlines, there followed a series of orders that confirmed Airbus as a serious contender in the global aircraft manufacturing industry.

1970

Airbus Industrie was formally established, marking the beginning of a new European aerospace consortium.

1972

The A300B made its first flight, showcasing Airbus's innovative approach to aircraft design.

1980s | New Stars Are Born

During the 1980s, Airbus expanded its product line and continued to innovate with new technologies. The introduction of the A320, with its advanced fly-by-wire technology, marked a significant milestone in aviation history, setting new standards for efficiency and safety.

1982

Cockpit Advances

Delivery of the first A300 FFCC jetliner version, received by Indonesian airline Garuda, incorporating the Forward-Facing Crew Cockpit configuration, which enabled a two-pilot crew to fly the aircraft without the need for a flight engineer.

1982

First flight of A310

The “Airbus Family” began with the shorter-fuselage A310 and longer-range A300. Lufthansa and Swissair were the first carriers.

1987

Launch of the A320

TWith the groundbreaking Fly-by-wire technology, the A320 Family would go on to record spectacular sales success, becoming the best-selling jetliner aircraft family ever.

1985

The A320 was officially launched, introducing new levels of efficiency and automation.

1988

The A320 entered service, quickly becoming a popular choice for airlines around the world.

1990s | Global Expansion

The 1990s saw Airbus solidify its position as a major player in the global aerospace industry. With the launch of the A330 and A340 families, Airbus offered a range of aircraft that catered to various market segments, from long-haul to regional flights.

1991

arrival of the A340

The ultra long-range four engined A340-300, able to seat 295 in a three-class configuration and fly 7,150 nautical miles/13,200 kilometres, was rolled out in October, 1991.

1992

Maiden flight of the medium to long-range A330

Carrying 335 passengers in two classes, the A330 marked another milestone with the first Rolls-Royce engine – the Trent 700 - on an Airbus aircraft.

1996

Super jumbo A3XX

Airbus Industrie sets up Large Aircraft Division, to develop the super jumbo A3XX, later designated the A380.

1992

The A330 and A340 families were launched, offering greater range and capacity options.

1994

The A330 and A340 entered service, further establishing Airbus as a key player in the wide-body market.

2000s | A new Era Begins

The 2000s were a groundbreaking decade for Airbus, highlighted by the introduction of the A380, the world's largest passenger airliner. This period also saw Airbus continue to innovate with new models and technologies, solidifying its position as a leader in the aerospace industry.

2002

First flight tests of the A318, the smallest member of the A320 family

Captain Bernd Schaefer confirmed that the aircraft handled as anticipated on its maiden flight, which kicked off the A318 flight test campaign.

2005

Unveiling the A380

The world's largest and most modern passenger jet is unveiled to the public. Later that year it would complete its maiden flight. .

2007

First flight of the A330 MRTT

Based on the successful A330-200 airliner, the A330 MRTT is the benchmark for new-generation multi-role tanker/transport.

2005

The A380 made its maiden flight, capturing the world’s attention as the largest commercial aircraft ever built.

2007

The A380 entered commercial service, offering unparalleled passenger capacity and comfort.

2010s | We make it fly!

The 2010s were marked by significant advancements in technology and a continued expansion of Airbus’s aircraft lineup. The introduction of the A350 XWB and the continued development of the A320neo series underscored Airbus's commitment to innovation and environmental sustainability.

2011

A record Breaking year!

Beginning with a milestone order: Virgin America’s acquisition of 60 A320s, Airbus hits above its 10,000th order mark. Also in January 2011, Airbus signed a Memorandum of Understanding with IndiGo for 180 A320s.

2018

The newest members of Airbus’ single-aisle aircraft family

A milestone partnership between Airbus and Canada’s Bombardier took effect in July with Airbus acquiring a majority stake in the C Series Aircraft Limited Partnership.

2019

Growth of the A220

As of early 2019, more than 530 orders were placed for A220 aircraft. Airbus is to open a new assembly line for the aircraft at its U.S. manufacturing facility in Mobile, Alabama, which will build jetliners for U.S. customers.

2019

A321XLR: Efficiency and extra-long range

After its official launch at the 2019 Paris Air Show, Airbus’ extra-long-range single-aisle A321XLR steadily built up a strong base of orders and commitments from customers around the world.

2013

The Airbus A350 XWB made its maiden flight, introducing advanced aerodynamics, materials, and systems technologies.

2016

The A350 XWB entered service with airlines, setting new standards in passenger comfort, efficiency, and range.
